STGFormer: Spatio-Temporal GraphFormer for 3D Human Pose Estimation in Video

Yang Liu and Zhiyong Zhang Yang Liu, and Zhiyong Zhang are with the School of Electronics and Communication Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University, Shenzhen, Guangdong, China. Email: liuy2529@mail2.sysu.edu.cn; zhangzhy99@mail.sysu.edu.cn (Corresponding author: Zhiyong Zhang)
Abstract

The current methods of video-based 3D human pose estimation have achieved significant progress; however, they continue to confront the significant challenge of depth ambiguity. To address this limitation, this paper presents the spatio-temporal GraphFormer framework for 3D human pose estimation in video, which integrates body structure graph-based representations with spatio-temporal information. Specifically, we develop a spatio-temporal criss-cross graph (STG) attention mechanism. This approach is designed to learn the long-range dependencies in data across both time and space, integrating graph information directly into the respective attention layers. Furthermore, we introduce the dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN (MHR-GCN) module, which utilizes modulation to optimize parameter usage and employs spatio-temporal hop-wise skip connections to acquire higher-order information. Additionally, this module processes temporal and spatial dimensions independently to learn their respective features while avoiding mutual influence. Finally, we demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in 3D human pose estimation on the Human3.6M and MPI-INF-3DHP datasets.

I Introduction

3D human pose estimation predicts the coordinates of body keypoints in three dimensions from input images or videos. Compared to 2D pose estimation, it provides more comprehensive and accurate spatial information. In the field of computer vision, this technology has garnered widespread attention due to its potential applications in action recognition [1, 2], human-computer interaction [3], and autonomous driving [4, 5]. 3D human pose estimation can primarily be divided into single-stage methods and two-stage methods. Single-stage methods directly estimate the end-to-end 3D human pose from inputs; two-stage methods initially detect the coordinates of keypoints in images and then regress to a 3D human pose based on the detected 2D keypoints. Currently, research shows that the performance metrics of two-stage methods generally surpass those of single-stage methods, mainly due to the significant advancements in 2D pose estimation networks. For instance, ViTPose [6] utilizes a transformer as the backbone to extract features and employs a lightweight decoder for pose estimation. The proposed method has been demonstrated to be effective and flexible in 2D pose estimation. However, the second stage, which involves transitioning from 2D pose to 3D, remains unsatisfactory due to the ill-posed problem. This issue stems from the depth ambiguity inherent in estimating 3D coordinates from 2D, caused by a lack of depth prior information.

To address the aforementioned issues, a series of methods have been proposed that are based on backbones such as CNNs [7, 8, 9, 10], GCNs [11, 12, 13, 14, 15], and transformers [16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22]. For example, Einfalt et al. [19] perform temporal upsampling within Transformer blocks to process time-sparse 2D pose sequences, thereby reducing the modelâ€™s size while still generating time-dense 3D pose estimates. StridedFormer [22] reduces sequence redundancy by substituting the fully connected layers in the feedforward network of the Vanilla Transformer Encoder (VTE) with strided convolutions, thereby progressively decreasing the sequence length to lower computational costs. Although the use of transformers capitalizes on the advantages of scalable model capacity and high parallelism, which show promising prospects in enhancing the performance of pose estimation, several challenges still persist.

First, owing to the absence of structural priors in the transformer, its performance in 3D human pose estimation still trails behind the state-of-the-art methods based on GCNs. These graph-based methods effortlessly represent the human skeleton as a graph, where joints are regarded as nodes, and the bones between joints are considered as edges connecting these nodes. Currently, some improvements that integrate graphs with transformers have emerged [23, 24, 25]. For instance, STCFormer [25] introduces body structure graph information as inductive biases into the transformers. This strategy influences the versatility of the transformers, which are commonly regarded as structures with weak inductive biases, with significant implications for pre-training and the scalability of large models.

Second, spatio-temporal transformers excel in capturing long-range dependencies, whereas GCN architectures integrate information and inter-node relationships. Encoding graph information into the attention layer significantly enhances the modelâ€™s understanding of direct connections between nodes. Meanwhile, replacing MLP with GCN in transformer may amplifie the ability to aggregate information throughout the entire graph structure. Integrating these two approaches allows the model to process graph data with greater precision and efficiency, thus optimizing performance from different aspects. However, in the most of GCN architectures, the transformation matrices are shared among all nodes within the multi-layer convolutional filters, impeding the efficiency of information exchange among nodes. To address this issue, mechanisms such as weight unsharing [26] and weight modulation [27] have been introduced in the spatial dimension, yet these solutions have not been effectively extended to the temporal dimension.

Third, higher-order dependencies are often neglected in the processing of graph neural networks. Although some studies address these dependencies in the spatial dimension by extending the first-order neighbors to high-order neighbors [28, 29], they remain unresolved in the temporal dimension. This issue of temporal higher-order dependencies originates from the systemâ€™s dynamic characteristics, whereby the current state is influenced not only by the immediately preceding state but also by earlier states.

To overcome the limitations discussed above, this paper presents a framework that integrates spatio-temporal transformer with graph encoding and dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN for 3D human pose estimation. This framework not only captures spatio-temporal long-range dependencies but also elucidates the relationships between spatio-temporal nodes and their structures. We summarize the main contributions of this work as follows.

(1) We propose a spatio-temporal GraphFormer for 3D human pose estimation from monocular video using spatio-temporal criss-cross graph attention and dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN.

(2) We introduce a spatio-temporal criss-cross graph attention mechanism which encodes temporal and spatial graph structural relationships into attention layers.

(3) We design a dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN module and integrate it into our GraphFormer architecture.

(4) We demonstrate extensive experiments conducted on Human3.6M and MPI-INF-3DHP datasets and ablation studies that our proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance in comparison with strong baselines.

II Related Work

In recent years, graph convolutional networks and transformers have made significant progress in 3D human pose estimation. As previously mentioned, we implement 3D human pose estimation from image sequences or video inputs through a two-stage approach.

II-A GCN-based methods.

The skeletal structure of body can be naturally represented as a graph, hence there has been extensive research in image-based 3D human pose estimation using GCNs, which has yielded significant results [13, 15, 26, 27, 30, 31]. To more effectively address the ill-posed problem of lifting 2D joint keypoints into 3D space, additional temporal information is extracted from sequential frames combined with spatial information related to body structure. For instance, the Global-local Adaptive Graph Convolutional Network (GLA-GCN) [11] models the spatio-temporal structure using a graph representation and traces local joint features through individually connected layers. Zeng et al. [32] developed a hop-aware hierarchical channel-squeezing fusion mechanism to efficiently extract relevant information from neighboring nodes in GCNs. Hu et al. [33] represent the human skeleton as a directed graph to effectively reflect the hierarchical relationships among the nodes. Although graph-based methods effectively utilize the bodyâ€™s structural information, they encounter a significant limitation in terms of scalability. Adding more network layers does not enhance model performance, and even widely adopted residual connections yield minimal impact.

II-B Transformer-based methods.

In addition to GCN, transformer also used to model spatio-temporal correlations for video-based 3D human pose estimation [17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, 34, 35]. Specifically, MixSynthFormer [17] combines synthesized spatial and temporal attention to effectively integrate inter-joint and inter-frame significance. HSTFormer [18] captures multi-level joint spatial-temporal correlations, ranging from local to global, through four transformer encoders and a fusion module. Li et al. [22] utilized a vanilla transformer encoder to model long-range dependencies in 2D pose sequences, while a strided transformer encoder aggregates this long-range information into a single-vector representation, employing a hierarchical global and local approach.

The Transformer has high scalability and is good at capturing long-distance dependencies, but it lacks structural prior information. As a result, several methods have been developed that integrate graph and transformer. The Spatio-Temporal Criss-cross Attention (STC) [25] learns spatio-temporal features and incorporates the bodyâ€™s graph structure information as a biased input in the proposed framework. Zhai et al. [24] proposed the Hop-wise GraphFormer, which enhances GCN-based pose estimation networks by grouping joints according to k-hop neighborhoods and capturing potential joint correlations across various joint synergies. Zhao et al. [23] designed a novel model, GraFormer, that combines graph convolution and transformers to exploit relationships among graph-structured 2D joints.

Our work also belongs to the realm of 3D pose lifting that employs a fusion of transformer and graph. Unlike other approaches, our model integrates graph information directly into the attention layers, thereby significantly improving adaptability. Furthermore, it incorporates spatio-temporal hop-wise modulated GCNs within the transformer framework, effectively capturing high-order dependencies in both time and space. This approach complements the long-range dependencies typical of transformers, thus substantially enhancing the modelâ€™s capacity to represent complex data structures.

III-B Overall Architecture

Figure 1(a) provides an overview of the proposed framework, which mainly comprises three parts: a joint-based embedding, spatio-temporal criss-cross graph transformer blocks, and a regression head. The joint-based embedding projects the input 2D coordinates of each joint into the feature space. STGFormer blocks aggregate the spatio-temporal context and graph information, updating the representation of each joint. The 3D coordinates are estimated from the learnt features by a regression head.

Spatio-Temporal GraphFormer. The STGFormer block is derived from the traditional transformer block, replacing the original multi-head self-attention layer with spatio-temporal criss-cross graph attention (STG attention). IV-A Experimental Setup

1) Datasets and Evaluation Metrics:

Human3.6M [39] is the most widely used dataset in the evaluation of 3D human pose estimation. It encompasses a vast collection of 3.6 million poses captured using RGB and ToF cameras from diverse viewpoints within a real-world setting. Adhering to the widely used standard protocol, we set five subjects (S1, S5, S6, S7, and S8) for training, and two subjects (S9 and S11) for evaluation. We use Mean Per Joint Position Error (MPJPE) to measure the error under two protocols: Protocol 1 (referred to as P1) and Protocol 2 (referred to as P2). P1 involves calculating MPJPE between the estimated pose and the ground truth after aligning their root joints; P2 involves computing Procrustes-MPJPE (PA-MPJPE), where the ground truth and the pose prediction are further aligned through a rigid transformation.

MPI-INF-3DHP [40] is a large-scale dataset that offers over 2K videos featuring joint annotations of 13 keypoints in outdoor scenes, suitable for 2D and 3D human pose estimation. The ground truth was obtained using a multi-camera arrangement and a marker-less MoCap system. Here, we report the MPJPE, Percentage of Correct Keypoints (PCK) at a threshold of 150mm, and Area Under the Curve (AUC) results as the evaluation metrics.

2) Baseline Methods:

We evaluate the performance of our model by comparing it with state-of-the-art video-based 3D human pose estimation methods, including transformer-based approaches such as StridedFormer [22], MHFormer [21], MixSTE [20], P-STMO [41], HSTFormer [18], Einfalt et al. [19], and PoseFormerV2 [16], as well as GCN-based methods like UGCN [42] and GLA-GCN [11]. Additionally, we also compare our model with methods that combine graphs and transformers, such as STCFormer [25] and MotionAGFormer [43].

3) Implementation Details:

In the experiments, we utilize two kinds of 2D pose sequences as input including the estimated 2D pose by the pre-trained CPN [44] and the real 2D pose (ground truth). For model training, the network parameters are optimized for 40 epochs using the Adam optimizer, with a batch size of 128. The basic learning rate is 0.001, with a decay of 0.97 per epoch. We instantiate our architecture with L = 6 for transformer layers, F = 256 for feature embedding dimensions, H = 8 for self-attention heads, N = 2 for MHR-GCN layers. Our model is implemented using the PyTorch toolkit, version 2.1.0, and runs on RTX 4090 GPU with driver version 525.105.17 and CUDA version 12.0.

V Conclusion

In this paper, we introduce the Spatio-Temporal GraphFormer, a novel model that integrates long-range temporal-spatial dependencies with graph structural information in its attention mechanisms and utilizes a dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN Module to enhance the extraction of graph information for 3D human pose estimation in videos. Specifically, the spatio-temporal criss-cross graph attention separately models spatial and temporal correlations, leveraging encoded graph structure information within the model. The dual-path modulated hop-wise regular GCN Module adjusts graph weights and accounts for high-order dependencies across both time and space dimensions. Experiments on two benchmarks affirm the efficacy and superior generalization capability of our proposed model over state-of-the-art techniques. For future work, we plan to develop a multi-person skeletal sequence spatio-temporal graph representation method to extend our modelâ€™s applicability to 3D human pose estimation in multi-person scenarios and broaden its use in various contexts.
