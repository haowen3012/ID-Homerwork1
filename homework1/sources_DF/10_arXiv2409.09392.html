Topological Eigenvalue Theorems for Tensor Analysis in Multi-Modal Data Fusion

Ronald Katende
Abstract

This paper presents a novel framework for tensor eigenvalue analysis in the context of multi-modal data fusion, leveraging topological invariants such as Betti numbers. Traditional approaches to tensor eigenvalue analysis often extend matrix theory, whereas this work introduces a topological perspective to enhance the understanding of tensor structures. By establishing new theorems that link eigenvalues to topological features, the proposed framework provides deeper insights into the latent structure of data, improving both interpretability and robustness. Applications in data fusion demonstrate the theoretical and practical significance of this approach, with potential for broad impact in machine learning and data science.

Keyword:Tensor eigenvalues; Topological invariants; Betti numbers; Multi-modal data fusion; Algebraic topology

1 Introduction

The study of eigenvalues and eigenvectors in matrix theory is well-established; however, extending these concepts to tensors, particularly in the context of multi-modal data fusion, remains less developed [1]. Tensors, as higher-dimensional generalizations of matrices, are increasingly used to model complex, multi-way data structures in machine learning and data science [2, 3]. Current methods for tensor eigenvalue analysis typically extend matrix-based approaches [4, 5], leaving a significant gap in formulating algebraically rigorous frameworks that leverage the unique properties of tensors [1, 6]. This manuscript addresses this gap by introducing novel topological methods for analyzing tensor eigenvalues, particularly in multi-modal data fusion [2, 7]. Unlike traditional techniques, which rely on algebraic methods derived from matrix theory [8], our approach utilizes algebraic topology to establish new eigenvalue theorems that are independent of classical linear algebra. This work contributes to the theory of tensor eigenvalues by

    Proposing a topological framework for tensor eigenvalue analysis,

    Establishing new theorems linking tensor eigenvalues to topological invariants, and

  • â€¢

This work represents a significant theoretical advancement with practical implications for machine learning and data analysis.

4 Implications of Topological Invariance

Corollary 1 asserts that the eigenvalues of homotopy-equivalent tensors are identical. This result has significant implications for data fusion, as it guarantees the stability of the eigenvalue spectrum under continuous deformations of the underlying data [10]. In practical applications, where multi-modal data is often subject to noise [12], perturbations [13], or other continuous variations, such stability is critical. The corollary ensures that as long as the underlying topological structure of the data is preserved (i.e., the tensors are homotopy equivalent), the eigenvalues remain unchanged. This robustness increases the reliability of tensor-based methods in data fusion by protecting against small variations that might otherwise lead to incorrect interpretations [14].
