2.4 Auxiliary features

To enhance the performance of the deep learning method, we include four additional features that are relevant for describing ocean dynamics globally and regionally. First, we include steric sea level anomalies because their changes may indirectly reflect mass changes, especially in the regions where halosteric signals correlate with mass variationsÂ (JordÃ  and Gomis, 2013). Moreover, steric changes in the deeper parts of the ocean can induce mass changes on the shelf to balance horizontal pressure gradientsÂ (Bingham and Hughes, 2012; Woodworth etÂ al., 2019). Monthly steric heights were computed from EN4 subsurface temperature and salinityÂ (Good etÂ al., 2013), again using the GSW Oceanographic Toolbox. The EN4 profiles are based on ocean floater observations and provide information independent of other features. Second, we consider the fact that the impacts of atmospheric winds on ocean dynamics vary with the ocean depths, i.e., the response in shallow regions is generally more energeticÂ (Bingham and Hughes, 2008b). Therefore, additional features describing bathymetry and wind stress vectors are meaningful. We include the 1-arcminute ETOPO1 dataset for seafloor depths and monthly east/west instantaneous turbulent surface stresses from ERA5Â (Hersbach etÂ al., 2020).

We use the BPR data initially collected byÂ Macrander etÂ al. (2010) and further processed byÂ Schindelegger etÂ al. (2021).
