Reviewer #2

This manuscript considers data fusion in a Bayesian parameter estimation framework. The goal of Bayes-optimum fusion is to obtain the posterior distribution (based on the data of all sensors) at a central node. Two different fusion rules are considered. The first, dubbed the CIL rule, is Bayes-optimum under the assumptions that the local data of the sensors are conditionally independent and the likelihood functions of all sensors as well as a common (shared) prior are known by the central node. The second, dubbed the CIP rule, is a suboptimum rule otherwise known as ‚Äùproduct of experts,‚Äù which multiplies all the local posterior distributions and normalizes the result. Both fusion rules are not new; in particular, the CIL rule has been previously presented and derived in Theorem 18 of [36], which is however not mentioned by the authors.

The contribution of the manuscript is an analysis and comparison of the CIL and CIP fusion rules, with a focus on the Gaussian case. First, a quasi-closed-form expression of the Kullback-Leibler divergence between the CIL and CIP results is provided.
